Social deprivation substantially changes multi-structural neurotransmitter signature of social interaction

Adam Hamed & Miron B. Kursa

Table 1. Summary of identified significant associations. Rows correspond to compound levels or their ratios in the analyzed brain structures. Columns correspond to six classes of the test, respectively, correlations with the number of USV episodes (vocalization), effects of social deprivation (isolation), effects of social encounter (encounter), overall and in particular, as correlation with encounter length (stage), as well as effects of either short or long encounter alone. Cells contain either upward arrows denoting positive association (elevated level or ratio in a certain group, or positive correlation) or downward denoting negative association. The number of arrows mark p-value level: one for p<0.05, two for p<0.01, and three for p<0.001, respectively. Structure abbreviations, denote, respectively: Amyg – amygdala, Cpu – caudate putamen (striatum), Hipp – hippocampus, mPFC – medial prefrontal cortex, Nacc – nucleus accumbens, VTA – ventral tegmental area. Substance abbreviations, denote, respectively: 3-MT – 3-Methoxytyramine, 5-HIAA – 5-Hydroxyindoleacetic acid, 5-HT – Serotonin, Ala – Alanine, Asp – Aspartate, DA – Dopamine, DOPAC – 3,4-Dihydroxyphenylacetic acid, GABA – gamma-Aminobutyric acid, Gln – Glutamine, Glu – Glutamate, Gly – Glycine, HVA – Homovanillic acid, NA – Noradrenaline, Taurine – Taurine, 3-MT/DA – 3-MT to DA concentration ratio, 5-HIAA/5-HT – 5-HIAA to 5-HT concentration ratio, DOPAC/DA – DOPAC to DA concentration ratio, Glu/GABA – Glu to GABA concentration ratio, Glu/Gln – Glu to Gln concentration ratio, HVA/DA – HVA to DA concentration ratio.
